About Me

Nicholas Jap



Hi! My name is Nicholas Jap and I am currently a sophomore at Dublin High School. I want to be an engineer who can design products that will make people's lives easier. I love helping other people and becoming an engineer is something that will allow me to reach the people in need. I plan to explore the engineering department and learn everything it means to be an engineer. I am interested in this field because I want to make a difference and use my creativity to design something that will be impactful for the world.

Some of the key skills that engineers have include being determined and hardworking. I am always determined to finish a task no matter how hard it is. I am also always determined as I never give up or lose hope, even during the bleakest situations. There are times when I could have stopped and just give in but I persevere in these moments. When I am losing in a chess match, I don't give up until the end and sometimes I end up coming back to win. I am also hard working because during group projects I contribute my very best and do more than I am assigned. When we get time to work I am always striving to get a lot of work done and focus upon the given task. This is my second year at Dublin High and I have been given the opportunity to participate in club activities like GiveLight and Magic Club. In these clubs, I learn ways I can benefit the community through doing simple magic for the ones in need or doing community service and helping the open kitchen. Additionally, I have also volunteered in the East Bay Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals(SPCA) shelter and this experience has taught me how to manage and work in a habitat environment. Outside of school, I have gotten various awards for piano and also managed to get 1st place in my first chess tournament. Being in clubs like chess, I have learned how to be patient and think ahead. In chess, being able to remain patient and being able to think one step ahead is crucial to winning the match.

I have also taken an engineering club back in 7th grade and this experience allowed me to understand basic coding and how to work with a team. I learned the basics of python and how to use a raspberry pi. Later on that year, we did a project where we built a race car and coded it to make it move in all directions. This was a group project and this project taught me how to work as a team. Additionally, I also learned how to think outside the box and listen to my team. I think overall, I am a well-rounded student who is always looking forward to new things and exploring new fields. I am someone who is learning how to be a better student but more importantly a better person. Thanks for reading and enjoy the rest of my site!
